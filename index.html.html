The Rhineland Crisis, 1936 — Cabinet Simulation

You are entering the French cabinet room in March 1936. German troops have crossed into the demilitarized Rhineland in open violation of Versailles and Locarno. Your first decision will shape the next diplomatic moments. This extended simulation is designed to help students think through deterrence, appeasement, balance of power, and the gradual collapse of the interwar order.

Strategic Briefing

Historical situation: On 7 March 1936, Hitler remilitarized the Rhineland. The move was dangerous: German troops had orders to withdraw if they encountered serious French military resistance. Yet France faced domestic political division, uncertainty about British support, and a deep fear of triggering another major war.

Teaching question: Should states defend treaty law immediately, even at the risk of escalation, or avoid war and preserve short-term peace? Your decision will trigger new events, fresh diplomatic papers, and newspaper reactions.
